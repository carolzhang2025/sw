Controller Gatewayy

Integrate diverse industrial protocols for effortless cloud connectivity and IIoT transformation.

What is CGW ?

CGW serves as the communication backbone for industrial environments. Featuring a high-performance embedded architecture with up to 16 serial ports and dual Ethernet interfaces, it simultaneously drives over 15 mainstream industrial protocols. By unifying diverse brands and inconsistent communication specifications across on-site PLCs, sensors, and meters, CGW standardizes device data onto a single, unified platform—building a robust foundation for IIoT connectivity.

Designed to address digital transformation challenges, CGW adopts a "Non-PC architecture" that eliminates driver failures caused by OS updates and complex DCOM configurations. Its intuitive web-based interface allows engineers to complete data mapping effortlessly without programming. Coupled with powerful self-diagnosis and communication logging, CGW ensures precise troubleshooting and high data availability, providing the most stable and reliable bridge to a smart factory.

Features

Universal Protocol Integration

Supports 15+ industrial protocols like Mitsubishi, Siemens, and AB, bridging communication gaps across diverse equipment.

Browser-Based Configuration

Configuration via web browser eliminates software installation, significantly streamlining on-site deployment.

Standalone Embedded Architecture

Non-PC architecture avoids risks from OS updates or DCOM issues, ensuring superior system stability.

Flexible Hardware Scalabilit

Dual Ethernet and up to 16 serial ports, with optional 4G/Wi-Fi for versatile networking and cloud connectivity.

Smart Diagnostics & Logging

Real-time monitoring and built-in logging enable precise troubleshooting and higher maintenance efficiency.

Precision Time Sync & Calibration

Built-in NTP calibration ensures synchronized time-stamping across systems, guaranteeing data accuracy for analysis.

How CGW works

In the architecture of smart manufacturing, the efficiency of data flow directly determines the quality of decision-making. The Smart Gateway CGW (Controller Gateway) acts as a vital bridge between the industrial field and upper-tier systems. Through high-performance conversion and transmission mechanisms, it transforms fragmented device protocols into standardized information streams. The following four key steps demonstrate how CGW helps enterprises build a stable data foundation, seamlessly completing the journey from device connectivity to digital transformation.

01

Downstream Data Collection

CGW connects downstream to various field devices and control systems to collect and consolidate real-time data from the source.

02

Protocol Normalization

The built-in engine normalizes over 15 industrial protocols into standardized formats, eliminating communication barriers between diverse equipment.

03

Upstream Integration

CGW converts and publishes data into standard interfaces (e.g., OPC UA, MQTT, SECS), making it seamlessly available for upper-layer systems and the cloud.

04

Scalable 2-Tier Architecture

Construct a 2-tier architecture for distributed data collection across multiple sites while maintaining efficient, centralized data management.

Customer story

Sub-Fab Equipment Integration: Optimizing Monitoring for 2,000+ Dry Pumps and Scrubbers

In semiconductor manufacturing, Dry Pumps and Scrubbers are vital for production continuity. As an industry leader, the customer prioritizes the operational efficiency of these systems and furnace tubes while optimizing data visualization to safeguard high yields and long-term operational resilience.

With over 2,000 devices in the Sub-Fab, 24/7 continuous monitoring is mandatory. This project establishes a stable, scalable IIoT communication core to unify data from massive devices and prevent unplanned production downtime.

To diversify risks, the customer utilizes multiple brands of Dry Pumps and Local Scrubbers, resulting in fragmented and incompatible communication protocols. With over 2,000 devices forming a massive system on-site, the resulting data load has severely overwhelmed monitoring PCs, leading to frequent disconnections and preventing maintenance teams from accessing real-time operational status. As production scales up, the customer faces two critical challenges :

  • Protocol Fragmentation from Multiple Vendors

    While multi-vendor sourcing mitigates supply chain risks, it creates a landscape of incompatible protocols, making unified plant-wide data acquisition extremely difficult.

  • Massive Connectivity Overloading PCs

    Directly connecting over 2,000 devices to monitoring PCs causes CPU overload from massive data packets. This leads to frequent system crashes, preventing the acquisition of real-time equipment status.

The customer implemented CGW as the plant-wide core, replacing centralized loads with a distributed architecture for highly reliable monitoring.

  • High-Efficiency Edge Acquisition

    Connects directly to Dry Pump and LSC controllers via CGW for stable, real-time data collection.

  • System Load Offloading

    Uses CGW as a relay to reduce direct PC-to-machine connections, offloading the CPU burden from the host.

  • Protocol Integration

    Consolidates diverse protocols into a unified platform, enhancing connection quality and data consistency.

CGW optimizes the communication architecture, transforming fragile monitoring links into a highly resilient and scalable digital management system.

  • Extreme Stability

    Resolves overload-induced disconnections, ensuring 24/7 stable operation and zero data loss.

  • Seamless Integration

    Unifies multi-brand protocols for single-platform monitoring of heterogeneous devices.

  • Maintenance Efficiency

    Lowers crash risks by replacing complex PC setups, enabling rapid onboarding via pre-configured modules.

"CGW solved our disconnection issues and established forward-looking digital resilience for the entire plant." — Project Lead
